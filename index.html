Tribute to Subhash Chandra Bose

Biography

Subhash Chandra Bose, also known as Netaji, was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha. Bose was known for his radical approach and was the founder of the Indian National Army (INA).

Subhash Chandra Bose was a brilliant student and passed the Indian Civil Services examination. However, he resigned from his position and joined the Indian National Congress. He became the President of the Congress in 1938 and 1939 but later parted ways due to differences with Mahatma Gandhi and other Congress leaders.

During World War II, Bose sought the help of Germany and Japan to free India from British rule. He formed the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) and led it against the British forces. His famous slogan was "Give me blood, and I will give you freedom!"

Subhash Chandra Bose's efforts played a significant role in India's struggle for independence. He is remembered as a brave and determined leader who fought tirelessly for his country's freedom.

Timeline

  • 1897: Born in Cuttack, Odisha.
  • 1919: Passed the Indian Civil Services examination.
  • 1921: Resigned from the Indian Civil Services and joined the Indian National Congress.
  • 1938: Became President of the Indian National Congress.
  • 1941: Escaped to Germany to seek support for India's independence.
  • 1943: Formed the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) in Singapore.
  • 1945: Allegedly died in a plane crash in Taiwan.

Notable Quotes

"Give me blood, and I will give you freedom!"
"One individual may die for an idea, but that idea will, after his death, incarnate itself in a thousand lives."
"It is blood alone that can pay the price of freedom. Give me blood and I will give you freedom!"
